<p>
     As a Senior QA Engineer your primary mission will be to drive customer confidence by assuring the quality of kCura’s current and future software products.  You will contribute to a motivating Agile environment that empowers teams and individuals to engineer QA efficiencies in practice, through process and with effective tools/techniques, in a brand new, startup-style kCura office in Krakow, Poland.
    </p>
<b>
<u>
      Responsibilities:
     </u>
</b>
<div>
<p>
      Senior QA Engineer is responsible for delivering results for the Product Development department by:
     </p>
<ul>
<li>
       Identifying and removing risks to quality for kCura’s software products
      </li>
<li>
       Contributing to a Scrum team that solves complex challenges and builds working software
      </li>
<li>
       Raising the collective QA and Development knowledgebase of a product development team
      </li>
<li>
       Providing functional and QA expertise through instruction of team members on how to test our software
      </li>
<li>
       Identifying and resolving software inconsistencies, ambiguities and defects during the design and implementation phase of software building
      </li>
<li>
       Designing, coding, and documenting automated test cases within a defined framework to assure the quality of a functional domain
      </li>
<li>
       Translating existing manual regression test cases into automated tests
      </li>
<li>
       Integrating automated test cases in a continuous integration process
      </li>
<li>
       Defining improved standards and practices for writing software to test software
      </li>
<li>
       Carrying out root cause analysis on defects to ensure fixes are happening in the right way
      </li>
<li>
       Ensuring proper communication on risks to quality
      </li>
<li>
       Improving and adhering to standard operating procedures and the collection of measures put in place to ensure consistency and improvement in the way we test software
      </li>
<li>
       Contributing to the planning process to establish tasks and forecasts for work to be performed
      </li>
<li>
       Asking the right questions, in the right way, at the right time to reduce the introduction of quality risk
      </li>
<li>
       Being an ambassador for Agile QA by practicing principles such as TDD, ATDD and alike
      </li>
</ul>
